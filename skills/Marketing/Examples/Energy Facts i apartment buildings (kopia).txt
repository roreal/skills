Energy Facts i apartment buildings

https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/19401493.2024.2416684#d1e138

Tenants add about 2 g of moisture per kg of air to the indoor air in apartment, often fluctuating between 1 and 3 g/kg of dry air during the day.
The specific energy use for a new energy-efficient apartment building in the Stockholm area is around 60 kWh/m2,year.
Apartment buildings built about year 2000 years ago, their energy use can be 100–120 kWh/m2,year (which gives 75–95 kWh/m2,year without Hot Tap Water).
The total heat demand is approximately 25 kWh/m2,year.
This heat demand is divided between reheating the supply air in the AHU, which uses approximately 6 kWh/m2,year to maintain the desired supply air temperature.
The radiator system, which covers the heat demand for losses through the building envelope, which uses approximately 7 kWh/m2,year.
The split between the AHU and the the radiator system depends on the airflow balance and the supply air temperature.
In addition, the heat demand for window ventilation is estimated at 4 kWh/m2,year and is covered by the radiator system.
The consumption of hot tap water in apartment buildings is standardized at 25 kWh/m2
The normal use of household electricity is 30 kWh/m2.
The normal indoor temperature in apartment buildings in Sweden during the heating season is 21°C.
The heat loss per metre of hot water pipe with 60 mm insulation is 50 kWh per metre of pipe per year
